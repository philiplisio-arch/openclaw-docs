- **Multiple Companies Report H1 2026 Net Profit Forecasts** · covered by 4 outlets
 - Hengtong Co. (恒通股份; a Chinese logistics and port operator) expects H1 net profit of 1.66–2.03 billion yuan, up **66.94%–104.03%** year‑on‑year, driven by new port berths at subsidiary Shandong Yulong Port, according to the company. [National Business Daily, 8 Jul 2026](http://www.nbd.com.cn/articles/2026-07-08/4461026.html).
 - BOE A (京东方A; a Chinese display panel maker) expects H1 net profit of 5.00–5.50 billion yuan, up **54%–69%** year‑on‑year, with Q2 net profit estimated at 3.293–3.793 billion yuan (up 92%–122% quarter‑on‑quarter), driven by resilient display demand and flexible AMOLED growth, according to the company. [Finance.eastmoney.com, 8 Jul 2026](http://finance.eastmoney.com/a/202607083798532431.html).
 - Bohai Leasing (渤海租赁; a Chinese aircraft leasing company) expects H1 net profit of 3.0–3.6 billion yuan, a significant year‑on‑year increase, including non‑recurring debt restructuring gains of about 1.6 billion yuan, according to the company. [Finance.china.com.cn, 8 Jul 2026](http://finance.china.com.cn/stock/ssgs/20260708/6315283.shtml).
 - Shengxin Lithium Energy (盛新锂能; a Chinese lithium products manufacturer) expects H1 net profit of 1–1.2 billion yuan, turning from a loss of 841 million yuan a year earlier, due to lithium‑salt volume and price increases and full capacity release at its Indonesian plant, according to the company. [Jiemian, 8 Jul 2026](http://www.jiemian.com/article/14725519.html).

- **ST Cuihua Faces Delisting Risk After Annual Report Delay, Store Closures** · covered by 1 outlet
 - ST Cuihua (a Chinese jewelry retailer) was placed under delisting risk warning from July 7, with its ticker changed to *ST Cuihua, according to the company. [Securities Times, 8 Jul 2026](http://www.stcn.com/article/detail/4005377.html).
 - The company and its subsidiaries had cumulative overdue borrowings of **951 million yuan** as of June 30, and 45 major bank accounts were frozen due to contract disputes and overdue borrowings, according to company filings. [Securities Times, 8 Jul 2026](http://www.stcn.com/article/detail/4005377.html).
 - The number of direct‑sale stores dropped from 24 in 2021 to **13** by end‑June 2025, and its largest direct‑sale store ‘Zonghao’ has ceased operations, according to the company. [Securities Times, 8 Jul 2026](http://www.stcn.com/article/detail/4005377.html).
