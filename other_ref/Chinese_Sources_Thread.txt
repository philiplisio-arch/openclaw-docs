Our recent conversation focused on how to substantially increase your Chinese media and social media inputs for OpenClaw while keeping costs near zero. You are looking for a way to make the "Signal Layer" more valuable without paying for expensive, gated enterprise APIs.

The options we discussed fall into three categories:

### 1. The "Open-Source Bridge" (RSSHub)
This was the primary recommendation for a "high-ROI" upgrade.
* **What it is:** RSSHub is an open-source generator that turns almost any Chinese site (WeChat Official Accounts, Weibo, Bilibili, Zhihu) into a standard data feed.
* **How it helps you:** It bypasses the need for expensive official APIs. You can run it on your own VPS for **$0**, and it provides a steady stream of data from the exact accounts you want to monitor.
* **Implementation:** We estimated a **1–3 hour setup time**. You can run it on the same VPS as OpenClaw using Docker, and use a browser extension (RSSHub Radar) to "discover" and add new Chinese sources in seconds.

### 2. The "Discovery Hack" (DeepSeek + Specialized Actors)
Instead of searching "blindly," we discussed using a cheaper, smarter "Discovery Layer."
* **DeepSeek/Qwen for Strategy:** Because Chinese LLM APIs are currently in a price war (up to 90% cheaper than Western models), you can use them as a "Search Router." You ask the model what is trending on Weibo *first*, then have your system go fetch those specific URLs.
* **Apify Actors:** For deep dives into platforms like **Xiaohongshu (Red)** or **Weibo**, we identified "Actors" (pre-built scrapers) that cost as little as $1 per 1,000 posts. This is significantly cheaper than a monthly SaaS subscription like Meltwater.

We reviewed how this fits into your existing **Foundation Doc** and **Architecture Map**:
* **The Moat:** By adding these sources, you are strengthening your competitive advantage: the ability to bridge the gap between Chinese-native insights and Western PR strategy.
